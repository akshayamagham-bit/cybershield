🧠 Social Engineering

Learn how attackers manipulate people to steal sensitive information

📌 What is Social Engineering?

Social engineering is a psychological attack where cybercriminals trick users into revealing confidential information such as passwords, OTPs, or banking details. Instead of hacking systems, they exploit human behavior like trust, fear, or urgency.

⚠ Why is it Important?

  • Protects personal and financial information
  • Prevents identity theft and fraud
  • Helps avoid scams and data leaks
  • Improves awareness against cyber attacks

❌ Types of Social Engineering Attacks

  • Phishing: Fake emails or websites to steal login details
  • Vishing: Fraud calls pretending to be bank officials
  • Smishing: Fake SMS with malicious links
  • Baiting: Offering fake rewards to trick users

📊 Real-Life Examples

  • "Your bank account is blocked, click here"
  • "You won a lottery! Claim now"
  • "Share OTP to verify your account"

🚨 Warning Signs

  • Urgent messages asking for immediate action
  • Unknown links or attachments
  • Requests for OTP or passwords
  • Spelling mistakes in emails/messages

✅ Best Practices

  • Never share OTP or passwords
  • Verify sender before trusting
  • Avoid clicking suspicious links
  • Enable two-factor authentication

💡 Pro Tip

Always pause and verify before taking action. Attackers rely on urgency — taking a moment to think can prevent major cyber attacks.
